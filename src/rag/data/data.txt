1. AHRI SUPPORT AND CERTIFICATION PROCEDURES
================================================================================

**Overview**

The AHRI (Air-Conditioning, Heating, and Refrigeration Institute) certification process applies to manufacturers seeking to verify the performance of their heat exchangers. The process requires strict adherence to AHRI standards and involves several steps, including application submission, testing, and verification.

**Qualification Process for OEM Applicants**

OEM applicants must submit a certification application package that includes:
- Physical coil data per ASHRAE 33
- One test report per Basic Model Group (BMG)
- The applicable Rating Calculation AHRI 410 form
- A copy of the applicant's selection software

The process involves:
1. Processing the application
2. Signing a certification agreement
3. Paying participation and licensing fees within 30 days
4. Selecting test samples

**Testing Requirements**

The number of tests required is 30% of the applicant's BMGs, with a minimum of two models. For example, if an applicant has 11 BMGs, 30% is 3.3, which rounds up to 4 tests. Samples must be available for testing within 30 days of AHRI's request.

**Testing and Certification**

AHRI contracts an independent third-party laboratory to conduct tests according to the standard. The laboratory compares measured results with the declared data. 

Compliance criteria:
- If the deviation in capacity exceeds 5%, the coil is considered non-compliant
- If pressure drops exceed 10%, the coil is considered non-compliant
- If either occurs, the model fails certification

**Certification Outcomes**

If the coil passes:
- It is certified and can be listed in the AHRI Directory

If it fails, the applicant has three options:
1. Repeat testing with a second identical sample (up to two tests)
2. Declare the model obsolete and discontinue production
3. Apply for rerating, which involves downgrading the BMG based on laboratory results

**Rerating (Downgrade) Process**

Rerating is not a recalibration but a downgrade of the certification. The software's rated values are adjusted proportionally to the measured laboratory data. 

Example:
- If the declared capacity is 100 kW but the laboratory measurement is 94 kW
- A ratio of 0.94 is computed
- The software must then be updated to reflect this new capacity
- All models within the BMG are downgraded accordingly

The recalibration involves:
- Printing the selection of the failed model
- Comparing calculations from the original and rerated software versions
- Updating the software with the rerated values and release number

This process ensures the software's output aligns with laboratory results, maintaining certification compliance.

**Preparing for Certification**

To minimize rerating risks, it is recommended to:
- Conduct testing in an accredited laboratory
- Prepare detailed test reports for each BMG
- Use UNILAB to calibrate the software geometry based on test data
- Keep the software builds aligned with AHRI requirements
- Involve UNILAB early in the process for calibration and setup

**Customer Cooperation and Communication**

Customers must promptly inform UNILAB of the start of certification or re-rating processes. If AHRI contacts the company directly for re-audits, the customer must communicate this to UNILAB with:
- Setup details
- Software version
- Other relevant information

Failure to communicate can lead to delays or certification issues.

**Additional Services and Support**

UNILAB offers services to prepare setup files, calibrate software, and support ongoing certification activities. These services include:
- Creating dedicated software setups for AHRI compliance
- Installing setups into the AHRI Virtual Machine
- Performing periodic checks and recalibrations
- Assisting with re-rating procedures if necessary

**Technical Notes on Software and Calibration**

- The current software version must include the geometries for certification
- Special versions of COILS are prepared for AHRI performance checks
- Calibration of bypass factors and other parameters may require detailed analysis of annual reports and test data
- In case of discrepancies, geometry calibration or detailed analysis may be performed at additional cost

2. AUTOMOTIVE SUITE: PAF, TAF, PAF3, ALOA
================================================================================

**Overview**

The automotive suite includes specialized tools such as PAF3, which can model three fluid flows in a heat exchanger with a common air side.

**PAF3 Capabilities**

PAF3, developed for IRA radiatori, allows for up to four sections with different fluids, such as:
- Water
- Glycol
- Oil
- Air

The interface enables:
- Defining multiple sections, each with its own fluid type and fin configuration
- Dividing the air flow proportionally based on the total airflow

Example: With a total airflow of 30,000 m³/h, each of the three sections could handle approximately 10,000 m³/h.

**ALOA Geometry Details**

The ALOA heat exchanger geometry includes parameters such as:
- Tube spacing (Tx - TubeSpace): 15.875 mm
- Row spacing (Rx - RowSpace): 12.7 mm
- B0 (overall width): 9.525 mm
- B1 (inner width): 7.9375 mm
- Tube length (TI): 13.4112 mm
- Tube height (Th): 2.4892 mm
- Tube thickness (Tt): 0.1778 mm

These parameters are stored in the database, used in project configurations, and can be updated or saved as needed.

**Fin Types and Parameters**

Louvered Fins:
- Louver pitch ($L_{p}$): distance from one louver to the next
- Louver height ($L_{h}$): height of the louver
- Louver length ($L_{d}$): length of the louver
- Tube depth ($T_{d}$): length of the bar
- Louver angle ($\theta$): inclination of the louver relative to the fin's direction

Multiport Mini Channel:
Parameters:
- Width ($B_{l}$): length of the side containing the channels
- Height ($B_{h}$): height of the bar
- Wall thickness ($B_{wt}$): distance between channels
- Rib thickness ($B_{rt}$): distance from channels to the top/bottom of the bar
- Ports ($p$): number of channels

5. HEAT EXCHANGER DESIGN AND CALCULATION FEATURES
================================================================================

**Circuits and Tube Configuration**

Number of Circuits Formula:
Number of circuits = (Number of pipes × number of rows) / number of pipes in series

Definitions:
- Number of circuits: the number of tubes fed simultaneously in the heat exchanger
- Number of tubes in series: how many tubes the fluid passes through for each circuit

Example:
If a coil has 3 rows and 6 tubes per row, with 2 circuits:
- Fluid enters the collector
- Distributed to each circuit
- Passes through the tubes in series within each circuit

**Coil Structure**

Each coil has:
- Specific tube arrangement
- Characterized by a certain number of rows and tubes per row, forming a matrix
- A circuit is an isolated section of the coil with an input and an output
- Fluid enters the collector and is distributed to the circuits

**Water Pressure Drops and Derating Factors**

Derating factors affect the capacity of coils:
- Can modify the water side pressure drop (Δp)
- Third derating factor allows adjusting water side pressure drop
- Accounts for real-world conditions that differ from ideal assumptions

**Security Settings**

Location: Within geometry details section

Purpose:
- Prevent accidental modifications of coil configurations or derating factors
- Factors are editable by technicians
- Must intentionally access the section
- Click on "factor details"
- Voluntarily modify values
- No lock mechanism, but menu structure makes accidental editing unlikely

**Coils in Series (Enterprise Version)**

Feature: "Coils in Series" option

Capabilities:
- Connect multiple coils in series
- Simulate complex configurations (e.g., coil treating outdoor air before mixing with return air)
- Software can handle by adding all coils to the heat series

Process:
1. Use "Add Coil to Heat Series" button to include all relevant coils
2. Select each coil (e.g., "COIL2")
3. Input data in designated fields (highlighted in yellow and white)
4. Complete series by defining compressor or other components
5. Finalize configuration
6. Perform calculations by clicking "Calculate"
7. Results can be printed with output review options

**Margin Factors in Coils**

Purpose: Adjustable parameters to fine-tune heat transfer calculations based on experimental data

Available Factors:

1. Fin corrugation factor or slope:
   - Reflects ratio between heat transfer coefficient of corrugated finned coil and plain fin coil
   - Value of 1 indicates plain fin
   - Higher or lower values adjust the exchange coefficient

2. Tube side coefficient factor:
   - Represents ratio of actual internal tube heat transfer coefficient to ideal
   - Value of 1 corresponds to smooth cylindrical tubes

3. Correction factor of friction:
   - Adjusts internal tube friction factor
   - Influences pressure drops inside tubes

4. Friction factor air side:
   - Modifies external air side pressure loss

5. Surface reduction/increase factor:
   - Accounts for differences in exchange surface area
   - Such as in coils with variable fin heights or spacing

6. Reduction area pass. tube:
   - Considers areas where fluid passage is reduced
   - Values from 0 to 1

7. Latent air friction factor:
   - Specific to dehumidification applications
   - Adjusts pressure drop caused by water accumulation on fins

8. Tube internal surface ratio:
   - Applicable to grooved tubes
   - Specifies ratio of internal grooved surface to smooth surface

9. Security factor:
   - Applies margin to overall calculation
   - Increasing or decreasing predicted performance

10. Sensible factor:
    - Adjusts sensible (temperature-related) capacity of coil
    - Typical default: 0.6

Important Notes:
- Factors are based on laboratory tests
- Should be used cautiously to avoid unphysical results

**Modeling Coils with Special Configurations**

Angular Cut Coils:
- Recommended to model as standard finned coil with appropriate dimensions
- Example: coil with length 1950 mm, 12 tubes per row, 7 rows, one inlet and one outlet collector

Unequal Row Lengths:
- Configuration with rows of unequal length (e.g., one full row and second row at 50% length) not directly supported
- UNILAB coils assume uniform row lengths
- Workaround: model as multiple segments or use custom configurations

**Reference Literature**

Recommended books for coil design:
- Hewitt, "Process Heat Transfer"
- Hewitt, "Heat Exchanger Design Handbook"
- Kays, "London, Compact Heat Exchangers"

**Overheating Definitions**

Evaporator Overheating:
- Temperature difference between fluid at compressor inlet and evaporator outlet

Condenser Overheating:
- Temperature difference between overheated fluid (at compressor outlet) and condensation temperature

**Surface Corrugation Types**

Terms describing types of surface corrugations:

1. Sine Wave fin:
   - Fin with sinusoidal corrugation pattern

2. Herringbone:
   - Tube surface with herringbone pattern of corrugations

3. Grooved tube (Gross Grooved):
   - Tube with internal grooves for improved heat transfer

Purpose:
- Enhance heat transfer by increasing surface area and turbulence

**Condensing Modes**

Partial or Multiphase Condensing:
- Divides process into: overheating, condensing, and subcooling phases
- Allows more detailed modeling
- Available only in Professional and Enterprise versions

Standard Condensing Mode:
- Software assumes 100% condensation capacity
- Entire refrigerant is condensed
- No partial or multiphase effects

6. MANIFOLD MANAGEMENT AND SELECTION
================================================================================

**Manifold Management Overview**

The software allows users to insert or select manifolds with specified dimensions and materials.

**Automatic Length Calculation**

Manifold length calculated based on coil dimensions and configuration:
- Vertical manifolds: length = coil height + 200 mm
- Horizontal manifolds: length = coil length + 200 mm

**Main Calculation Interface**

User options:
- Choose manifold material
- Select calculation mode (automatic or manual)
- Specify arrangement (vertical or horizontal)

Software capabilities:
- Calculates optimal manifold dimensions
- Allows manual input for testing specific configurations
- Specify number of tubes, skipped tubes, and circuits
- Determines fed tube count and pressure drops

**Automatic Manifold Selection**

Features:
- Automatic selection and sizing based on fluid velocity criteria
- Ensures pressure drops do not adversely affect calculation accuracy

**Calculation Process by Mode**

The software evaluates fluid velocity in manifolds relative to velocity inside tubes.

General Process:
1. Computes fluid velocity in each manifold
2. Compares with tube's internal velocity
3. If velocity in manifold ≤ velocity inside tubes:
   - Pressure drops considered negligible
   - Manifold selected as best fit

**Selection Criteria by Mode**

Cooling and Heating Modes:
- Inlet manifolds: minimum external diameter such that velocity ≤ tube velocity
- Outlet manifolds: same diameter as inlet manifolds

Condensing Mode:
- Considers gas velocity in manifolds
- Inlet manifolds: gas velocity ≤ tube velocity
- Outlet manifolds: same diameter as inlet manifolds

Direct Expansion Mode:
- No specific calculation for inlet manifolds
- Outlet manifolds: applies velocity comparison
- Note: Inlet manifolds for DX-Evaporators not calculable (consistent with Unilab Coils 8)

Steam Applications:
- Similar criteria to condensing mode
- Inlet manifolds: minimum external diameter based on gas velocity comparison
- Outlet manifolds: same diameter as inlet manifolds

Pump Evaporator Applications:
- Inlet manifolds: minimum external diameter such that liquid velocity ≤ tube velocity
- Outlet manifolds: equal to inlet manifolds

**Catalogue Integration**

- Software can automatically select manifolds from catalogues (e.g., Danfoss)
- Does not design distributors directly in direct expansion mode

**Design Principle**

Selection process ensures:
- Pressure drops in manifolds do not significantly impact overall calculation
- Reliable and optimized coil design

7. ADVANCED CALCULATION FEATURES AND PARAMETERS
================================================================================

**Capillary Pressure Drop Calculations**

Two-Phase Capillary Support:
- Software supports calculation of pressure drops in two-phase capillaries
- Considers phase change of fluids inside capillaries

Calculation Method:
- Determines length of capillary that produces specified pressure drop
- Given: flow rate and capillary diameter
- Uses inverse of typical pressure drop calculation

Pressure Drop Formula:
$$\Delta P = f \times \frac{L}{D} \times \frac{\rho v^2}{2}$$

Where:
- $\Delta P$ = pressure drop
- $f$ = friction factor
- $L$ = length of capillary
- $D$ = internal diameter
- $\rho$ = fluid density
- $v$ = fluid velocity

Microchannel Capillaries:
- For internal diameters ≤ 2mm
- Uses pre-developed models for microchannel flow
- Estimates pressure drops accurately

**Calibration of Moisture Effects**

Dehumidification and Condensation:
- Software allows calibration of moisture-related pressure drops
- Accounts for influence of water vapor condensation
- Effects on pressure drops across fins and coils
- Calibration based on laboratory tests

**Flow Arrangement Detection**

Automatic Detection:
- Software automatically determines flow mode based on tubes in series

Criteria:
- If tubes in series > 4: counterflow mode (uses counterflow equations)
- If tubes in series ≤ 4: crossflow mode (uses crossflow equations)

Note:
- Automatic detection simplifies user interaction
- Manual selection of flow type not available

**Report Generation**

Catalog Export:
- Software supports generation of detailed reports and catalogs
- Exportable Excel files
- Can compile coil performance data into structured catalog format
- Feature requires additional paid development time

**Refrigerant Management**

Available Refrigerants with Distributors:
- R134A
- R22
- R407C
- R410A
- R507A

Additional Features:
- Setting automatic licenses
- Adjusting fins per inch
- Hiding certain fields
- Customizing project paths
- Configuring database locations

**Measurement Units**

Unit Conversion:
- Can change global heat transfer coefficient units
- Example: from kg/(m²·h) to kg/(m²·°C)
- Accessible via tools menu
- Option to disable calculations based on DHML (Dynamic Heat Transfer Coefficient) method

**Vapor Quality**

Definition:
- Mass fraction of vapor in a saturated mixture

Formula:
$X = \frac{m_{vapor}}{m_{total}}$

Where:
- $X$ = vapor quality
- $m_{vapor}$ = mass of vapor
- $m_{total}$ = total mass of mixture

Interpretation:
- Vapor quality of 1: saturated vapor
- Vapor quality of 0: pure liquid

Impact on Performance:
- Vapor quality affects heat exchanger efficiency
- If vapor quality exceeds certain thresholds (e.g., 30%): may lead to suboptimal performance

Performance Improvements:
- Reduce temperature difference between evaporator and condenser
- Increase subcooling

**Additional Tools**

Available Features:
- Selecting distributors in coils
- Setting frost thickness
- Comparing different editions (Standard, Professional, Enterprise)

**Edition Comparison**

Enterprise Edition Features:
- Advanced energy recovery
- Heat balance calculations for multiple plants and coils
- Supports complex configurations:
  * Up to 10 plants
  * 50 coils per plant

Energy Recovery:
- Analyze multiple heat exchangers linked hydraulically
- Divide plant into zones
- Calculate heat exchange effectiveness
- Supports calculation of heat balance and energy recovery systems
- Example: bFly recuperators
